International Law and Policy

This research takes an interdisciplinary approach to understanding legal and political issues raised by the Syrian civil war. The primary sources reviewed in this research include Executive Branch documents, such as presidential statements and executive orders, as well as congressional materials, essentially House and Senate resolutions and federal laws.

International legal materials, comparatively the 1998 Rome Statute of the International Criminal Court will also be examined. Secondary materials to be analyzed are articles authored by legal and political science scholars. This investigation reveals the implications for peacemaking through international diplomacy. The insights, theories, and concepts used by legal and political science scholars will be integrated with the insights provided by the primary source materials in order to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the problems presented by American involvement in the Syrian conflict. The political aspects of this topic are integrated with legal and jurisdictional concerns of international law.
